The SiX Project

ABOUT

A Project About Drawing For
Social Re-Connectivity

SiX is a research project that explores how digital drawing can help patients with Locked-in Syndrome (LIS) reconnect socially and express themselves. The project uses the human body as an interface, converting minimal physical movements into drawing commands. This approach recognizes that creative expression is a fundamental human need, particularly important for those with severe motor impairments.


EVENTS / ACTIVITIES

Technology as a communication medium

The SiX project will generate a range of outputs that benefit both the design and healthcare communities, hosting workshops and presentations for students, researchers, and healthcare professionals.

1. Understanding LIS and its impact

2. Digital drawing as a communication tool

3. A Designing accessible and inclusive technologies

Exploring the potential of digital drawing to facilitate communication and expression for LIS patients and promoting the development of technologies that meet the needs of people with disabilities is our objective.
